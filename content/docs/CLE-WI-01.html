1 Purpose & Scope

This guide tells you HOW to find, select, appraise and weigh published evidence so it can be used in clinical evaluation (MDR) and in scientific-validity / clinical-performance sourcing (IVDR). It standardises the method that the Literature Search Protocol (CLE-TPL-04 / PEV-FOR-01) and Report (CLE-TPL-06 / PEV-TPL-03) record.

Principle: only rely on a study if it shares your device's INTENDED PURPOSE, POPULATION and CLAIM/HYPOTHESIS. Document everything so the search is reproducible (PRISMA).

3 Define the objective of the search

State which job the search does: (a) establish the state of the art; (b) provide clinical/performance evidence for THIS device; (c) support EQUIVALENCE to another device; or (d) feed safety / PMS. Each objective has different acceptance criteria.

5 Screen & select (the 'same scope/hypothesis' test)

Apply pre-defined inclusion/exclusion criteria. INCLUDE a study only if its population, intervention, comparator and outcome match your intended purpose and the hypothesis you are testing. Remove duplicates. Keep a PRISMA flow (records identified -> screened -> assessed -> included) with reasons for exclusion.

6 Appraise RELEVANCE

Rate how applicable each included source is to YOUR device and claim (device similarity/equivalence, indication, population, data type). Low-relevance sources carry little weight even if high quality.

7 Appraise QUALITY (methodology)

Guidance (delete before release): Weight by study design and rigour: RCT > controlled cohort > observational > case series > expert opinion. Assess risk of bias, sample size, statistical validity and completeness. Use a consistent appraisal scale.

8 Extract, synthesise & weigh

Extract the relevant endpoints; synthesise across sources; reconcile conflicting results; state the weighted conclusion.

9 Conclusion & traceability

Trace every clinical/performance claim to the appraised sources. Where evidence is insufficient, define the gap and route it to a clinical investigation (CLE-TPL-09) / performance study (PEV-TPL-13) or to PMCF/PMPF.

10 Document & update

Record the search in the protocol (CLE-TPL-04 / PEV-FOR-01) and report (CLE-TPL-06 / PEV-TPL-03). Re-run on the defined schedule and when significant new evidence appears.

11 Worked example

[e.g. Question: 'In adults with suspected condition X (P), does SaMD Y (I) vs radiologist read (C) achieve >=90% sensitivity (O)?' -> search PubMed+Embase 2015-2025, English; include only studies on the same indication and read-out; appraise; conclude sensitivity meets the SOTA benchmark or define a PMCF gap.]
