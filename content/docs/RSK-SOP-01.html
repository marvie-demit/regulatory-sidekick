HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT — guidance, delete before release
What this is (plain language)Risk management is how you find what could go wrong with your device, decide whether that is acceptable, reduce it, and keep watching after launch. Auditors treat the risk file as the backbone of the whole technical documentation - almost everything else (design, software, usability, clinical, security, AI) feeds into it.
When to use itOpen the risk file at project kick-off and keep it live until the device is withdrawn from the market. Re-open and update it at every design change and whenever post-market data (complaints, security findings, model drift) arrives.
Who needs itAppoint one named Risk Manager who owns the file. Developers, the Security Officer and Clinical contribute their analyses. Top Management must formally accept the overall residual risk and benefit-risk.
How to use it, step by step1) Complete the Risk Management Plan (RSK-TPL-01) first - it sets your acceptance criteria and matrix. 2) Fill the risk table (RSK-TPL-03): one row per hazard/threat/AI-risk, tagged by branch. 3) Estimate severity x probability using your scales. 4) Add and verify controls in the ISO 14971 order (design > protective measures > information for safety). 5) Conclude overall residual & benefit-risk in the report (RSK-TPL-04). 6) Feed PMS/security/AI data back in.
What 'good' looks like (what auditors check)Every hazard traces to a control AND to objective evidence the control works. Residual risk is justified against pre-defined criteria. Security (TIR57) and AI (AI Act Art 9) risks live in the SAME file, not separate registers. The file shows it is updated from post-market data. The benefit-risk conclusion is signed by management.
Common pitfalls to avoidTreating risk as a one-off document; keeping disconnected security/AI risk spreadsheets; writing controls with no verification of effectiveness; forgetting reasonably foreseeable misuse; not updating after launch.

1 Purpose & Scope

This procedure defines how [Organisation] establishes, implements and maintains a single, integrated risk management process across the entire product lifecycle, combining product safety (ISO 14971), product/information security (AAMI TIR57, IEC 81001-5-1) and artificial intelligence (EU AI Act Art 9, ISO/IEC 42001) into one risk management file.

Scope: all medical devices and medical device software developed by [Organisation], including AI/ML medical devices, throughout design and development, production and post-market. Purely financial/business risks are out of scope.

3.1 Risk management planning

InputProject initiation; draft intended use (DEV-TPL-01); applicable standards
Activity• Establish the Risk Management Plan (RSK-TPL-01): scope, device/versions, lifecycle phases.
• Assign roles and confirm competence per branch (incl. cybersecurity and AI competence).
• Define risk acceptance criteria, the probability x severity matrix, and the overall-residual-risk policy.
• Specify analysis methods per branch and the method for verifying control effectiveness.
• Plan how production/post-production information is collected and reviewed.
ResponsibleRisk Manager (R); Top Management (A)
Method / CriteriaISO 14971 §4.4; acceptance policy approved before analysis
Tools / TemplateRSK-TPL-01
Output / RecordApproved Risk Management Plan
ClauseISO 14971 §4

3.2 Risk analysis

InputPlan; intended use & foreseeable misuse; architecture; asset inventory (SEC-LIS-01)
Activity• Identify safety hazards, sequences of events, hazardous situations and harm, incl. use errors (HUF).
• Identify security assets, threats and vulnerabilities via threat modelling (TIR57).
• Identify AI risks: data bias, performance limits, drift, loss of oversight (AI Act Art 10/15).
• Estimate severity and probability per the plan's scales.
• Record each risk with its branch tag in the risk table (RSK-TPL-03).
ResponsibleRisk Manager (R); Process Owners & Security Officer (Support); Clinical (Consulted)
Method / CriteriaISO 14971 §5; TIR57; AI Act Art 10/15
Tools / TemplateRSK-TPL-03
Output / RecordCompleted analysis rows
ClauseISO 14971 §5

3.3 Risk evaluation

InputEstimated risks (RSK-TPL-03)
Activity• Compare each risk against the acceptance criteria.
• Document risks that meet the criteria as acceptable with justification.
• Flag and prioritise risks requiring reduction.
ResponsibleRisk Manager (R); Process Owners (Support)
Method / CriteriaAcceptance criteria & matrix per RSK-TPL-01
Tools / TemplateRSK-TPL-03
Output / RecordRisk evaluation
ClauseISO 14971 §6

3.4 Risk control

InputRisks requiring reduction
Activity• Select controls in the ISO 14971 priority order (inherently safe design > protective measures > information for safety).
• Apply the same hierarchy for security (TIR57) and include AI human-oversight measures (Art 14).
• Implement each control and verify implementation and effectiveness with objective evidence.
• Check controls introduce no new risks; resolve safety-vs-security conflicts and record the rationale.
ResponsibleProcess Owners (R); Risk Manager (A); Security Officer (Support)
Method / CriteriaISO 14971 §7; TIR57; AI Act Art 14
Tools / TemplateRSK-TPL-03; V&V records
Output / RecordVerified controls
ClauseISO 14971 §7

3.5 Overall residual risk & benefit-risk

InputVerified controls; residual risks
Activity• Evaluate the overall residual risk of the device as a whole.
• Have Top Management decide acceptability of overall residual and benefit-risk.
• Disclose significant residual risks in the accompanying information.
• Document the conclusion in the report (RSK-TPL-04).
ResponsibleRisk Manager (R); Top Management (A)
Method / CriteriaOverall criteria per RSK-TPL-01; benefit-risk per §8
Tools / TemplateRSK-TPL-04
Output / RecordRisk Management Report
ClauseISO 14971 §8

3.6 Production & post-production

InputPMS, security surveillance, AI drift data
Activity• Operate channels to collect post-market information (PMS-SOP-01, SEC-TPL-02, AIM-LIS-01).
• Assess new information for impact on the risk file.
• Update the file and controls via Change Management / CAPA when acceptable risk no longer holds.
• Review the file at planned intervals and at each significant change.
