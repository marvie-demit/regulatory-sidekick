1 Welcome - how to use this library

This library gives you a complete, ready-to-tailor Quality Management System (QMS) for a medical-device company. You do not need a consultant to stand it up: follow this handbook, work through the documents in the order below, and replace the [placeholders] with your own information. Every document contains a grey 'How to use this document' block explaining its purpose, who fills it in, how, and what an auditor will look for - delete that grey guidance before you release the document.

Start with the master index: the Document List (DOC-LIS-04) lists every document and the module it belongs to.

2 One system, many standards

Instead of separate systems for quality, security, privacy and AI, this is ONE management system. Shared processes (document control, audit, management review, CAPA, training, supplier management, risk) are written once and satisfy all the standards together. You only add the domain-specific procedures you need.

3 How the library is organised

Each module folder contains one process folder per SOP, holding that SOP and all its templates, forms and lists. Document IDs follow DOMAIN-CLASS-NN (see DOC-LIS-05). Classes: MAN/POL (manual/policy), SOP/WI (procedures), FOR/TPL/LIS (forms/templates/lists), REC (records).

4 Medical Device Stepwise Implementation (MDSI) - the 4-phase model

This library is implemented through Medical Device Stepwise Implementation (MDSI) - the same way the WHO LQSI tool (Laboratory Quality Stepwise Implementation) implements ISO 15189 in laboratories: as a staged maturity journey, not all at once. You work through FOUR phases in order and finish a phase before starting the next. Within each phase, the work is grouped by the 12 Quality System Essentials (the building blocks), and each phase advances every essential to a higher level of maturity. The end-point for ISO 13485 is MDR/IVDR certification (instead of laboratory accreditation).

Track your progress in the Medical Device Stepwise Implementation Tracker (QMN-LIS-01), which lists every step, the essential it belongs to, the exact NotJustAny document to create, the clause, and a status column.

PhaseFocus (adapted from WHO LQSI)What you achieve
Phase 1Ensure the core processes operate correctly and safelyDocument & record control, organisation & roles, basic competence and infrastructure, and the core design-build-release workflow running under basic control and safety.
Phase 2Control & assure quality and create traceabilityIntegrated risk management, full design controls and software lifecycle, verification & validation, security and data protection, CAPA and nonconforming-product control - with end-to-end traceability.
Phase 3Ensure proper management, leadership & organisationQuality policy & objectives, management review, internal audit, data analysis, supplier surveillance, change and regulatory monitoring - the system is governed and led.
Phase 4Continuous improvement & prepare for certificationPost-market surveillance, vigilance, PMCF/PMPF, clinical/performance evaluation, the technical file & Declaration of Conformity, then the Notified Body audit path to certification.

The 12 Quality System Essentials used as the building blocks: Documents & Records · Organisation · Personnel · Equipment/Infrastructure · Purchasing · Process Management · Information Management · Customer Focus · Assessment · Nonconforming-Event Management · Continual Improvement · Facilities & Safety.

5 Choosing your modules - the Device-Applicability Matrix

The QMS is one always-on Core plus switch-on modules selected by DEVICE CHARACTERISTIC (not by device-type name). Use the Device-Applicability Matrix (QMN-LIS-02): mark which characteristics your device has, and it tells you which module(s), standards and documents to activate. A device can match several characteristics at once - activate ALL that apply; they compose (e.g. an active implantable with AI software activates Active + Sterile/Biocompatibility + Software + AI together).

6 Roles you need (and how to combine them)

In a small company one person can hold several roles; keep ownership internal. Typical roles: Top Management (accountable, approves), QMR (runs the QMS), Risk Manager, Software Lead, Security Officer/CISO, DPO, AI Lead, RA Manager. A person may hold more than one role provided independence is preserved for review/audit.

7 The document lifecycle

All documents are created, reviewed (by someone other than the author), approved, published, periodically reviewed, and archived per DOC-SOP-01. Keep everything electronic in access-controlled folders; the system version history is your audit trail.

9 Keeping it alive

Review documents at least every 2 years (DOC-SOP-01); run changes through CHG-SOP-01; drive issues through CAPA (CAP-SOP-01); analyse data and review at management review (MRV-SOP-01); keep the risk and clinical/performance files current from post-market data (PMS/PMCF/PMPF). Monitor regulations via REG-SOP-01 and the external-documents register (DOC-LIS-06).

10 FAQ

Q: Do I need every document?

A: No. Use the always-on Core plus the optional modules your device qualifies for (see the Device-Applicability Matrix, QMN-LIS-02). Modules you do not need simply stay dormant for your device - they are not deleted.

Q: Can I delete sections?

A: Yes - sections marked optional, and any genuinely non-applicable requirement, provided you justify the exclusion.

Q: Where do I record real data?

A: In the forms, templates and lists (FOR/TPL/LIS). A blank one is a document; a completed one is a record.

Q: How lean can I go?

A: Lean = remove duplication and bloat, never remove a mandated requirement. This library is already at that floor.
