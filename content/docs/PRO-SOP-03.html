What & whyHow you prove a manufacturing process consistently produces conforming product - process validation (IQ/OQ/PQ).
When to useWhen a process output cannot be fully verified by later inspection (a 'special process'), and on significant process change.
WhoProduction/Quality owns the validation; the QMR approves the validation master plan.
How to use itWrite the validation master plan, then qualify Installation (IQ), Operation (OQ) and Performance (PQ) against acceptance criteria, and revalidate on change.
What auditors checkA validation master plan; IQ/OQ/PQ with acceptance criteria met; statistical rationale; revalidation on change.
Pitfalls & relatedPitfalls: validating once and never revalidating; no statistical basis. Related: PRO-TPL-02..07, PRO-SOP-01, ISO 13485 7.5.6.

1 Purpose & Scope

Guidance (delete before release): 2-3 sentences: what this ensures and where it applies.

This procedure defines how [Organisation] validates manufacturing processes whose output cannot be fully verified by later inspection or test, to demonstrate they consistently produce conforming product. It applies to such processes (for example sterilisation, sealing, moulding, automated assembly) and covers the validation master plan, IQ/OQ/PQ qualification and revalidation.
