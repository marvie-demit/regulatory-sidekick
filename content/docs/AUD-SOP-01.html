1 Purpose & Scope

This procedure ensures the management system is audited at planned intervals to verify conformity with all applicable standards and effective implementation, and that nonconformities drive correction.

Scope: all IMS processes and standards (ISO 13485 8.2.4, ISO 27001 9.2, ISO 42001 9.2). Supplier and unannounced audits are covered where relevant. The organizational stress test (step 3.6) is a complementary technique.

3.1 Audit programme

InputProcess list, risk, importance, prior results
Activity• Establish a risk-based annual audit programme covering all processes and standards.
• Set audit frequency by the risk and importance of each process.
• Account for results of previous audits.

Method / CriteriaFrequency by risk/importance
Tools / TemplateAUD-TPL-01
Output / RecordAudit programme

InputAudit programme
Activity• Define the audit scope, criteria (standards/clauses) and schedule.

• Assign an auditor who is independent of the area being audited.
ResponsibleQMR (R)
Method / CriteriaAuditor independent of audited area
Tools / TemplateAUD-TPL-02

3.3 Conduct audit

InputAudit plan
Activity• Gather objective evidence against the requirements through interviews, observation and document review.

InputAudit evidence

InputAudit report

Tools / TemplateAUD-TPL-03

3.6 Organizational stress test (complementary)

InputSelected high-risk processes
Activity• Optionally simulate adverse scenarios (key-person loss, supplier failure, security incident, recall, model drift).
• Observe how the IMS responds and where it breaks down, beyond conformity checking.
• Capture resilience findings and improvement actions.
ResponsibleQMR (R); Process Owners (Support)
Method / CriteriaScenario-based stress test (Lean Normalization)
Tools / TemplateStress-test scenarios
Output / RecordResilience findings; actions
