1 Purpose & Scope

This guide tells you HOW to justify relying on data from ANOTHER device (MDR equivalence) or from existing studies/literature (IVDR scientific validity), instead of generating all your own data - and when that is NOT acceptable.

Use it together with the Literature method (CLE-WI-01) and record the result in the Demonstration of Equivalence (CLE-TPL-07) or the Scientific Validity Report (PEV-TPL-04).

2 MDR equivalence (MDCG 2020-5)

Guidance (delete before release): Equivalence must be demonstrated on ALL THREE dimensions; if any one fails, equivalence cannot be claimed.

DimensionMust match
ClinicalSame clinical condition/purpose, site, population, severity/stage, and similar performance
TechnicalSimilar design, conditions of use, specifications, properties, principles of operation
BiologicalSame materials/substances in contact with the same tissues/body fluids for similar duration

You must also have sufficient ACCESS to the technical documentation of the equivalent device (e.g. a contract) - without access, equivalence is generally not acceptable for Class III / implantable devices.

3 IVDR scientific validity (Annex XIII)

Establish the association of the analyte with the clinical condition/physiological state. Sources: peer-reviewed literature, consensus/expert opinion, proof-of-concept studies, and clinical performance data. Record in PEV-TPL-04.

4 The 'same scope / same hypothesis' test

Guidance (delete before release): Before relying on an existing trial or dataset, confirm its intended use, population, comparator and outcome match yours (use the PICO frame in CLE-WI-01). Note any differences and justify why they do not invalidate the data.

5 Document the justification

State the claim being supported, the source(s), the equivalence/validity argument, residual differences and their impact.

6 When equivalence / existing data is NOT enough

If the three dimensions are not met, access is lacking, or the evidence is insufficient/low-relevance, generate your own data: a clinical investigation (CLE-TPL-09) for MDR or a clinical performance study (PEV-TPL-13) for IVDR.
