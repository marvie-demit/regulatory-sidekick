What & whyHow you report adverse events to the FDA (MDR / MedWatch) and conduct US recalls and corrections.
When to useWhen a US-reportable event or field action arises. Dormant until you target the US market.
WhoThe RA Manager owns FDA reporting; management authorises recalls.
How to use itAssess the event against 21 CFR 803 criteria, file MDR/MedWatch reports within the deadlines, decide and conduct recalls/corrections (21 CFR 806), and keep the MDR files.
What auditors checkTimely MDR reports; recall/correction decisions and records; linkage to complaints.
Pitfalls & relatedPitfalls: missing the FDA reporting clock; unreported corrections. Related: VIG-FOR-01, CMP-SOP-01, 21 CFR 803/806.

1 Purpose & Scope

Guidance (delete before release): 2-3 sentences: what this ensures and where it applies.

This procedure defines how [Organisation] reports adverse events to the U.S. FDA under Medical Device Reporting (21 CFR 803) and manages corrections and removals (recalls, 21 CFR 806) for devices marketed in the USA. It applies whenever a U.S.-reportable event or field action arises.
